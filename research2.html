Home Research Interests
    My prior and current research investigates the dynamics of classical and quantum systems, with a focus on stochastic processes. By melting the boundaries of quantum mechanics, statistical physics, and non-equilibrium systems, currently I aim to build a unified stochastic framework for addressing problems in open quantum system. Previously, I also explored integrable systems and fractional diffusion frameworks to study anomalous transport, offering insights into fundamental aspects of quantum and classical dynamics. I believe science is for exploration of ideas and continious learning, and I enjoy cross-disciplinary ideas as applied within the scope of physics. Generality of ideas or simple solvable models for complex phenomena encapsulates the flavour of my research.

Stochasticity in Quantum Dynamics
    Quantum dynamics subject to noise is an active field of research. My interest in quantum dynamics focuses on the interface of stochastic process, quantum dynamics and quantum measurement. My current focus of research is unravelling noisy dynamics in qubits and its statistical description going beyond the average dynamics. Some related keywords are: open quantum system, stochastic operator variance, quantum trajectories, non-Hermitian dynamics, quantum correlations .

Classical Integrable Systems and its Hydrodynamics
    Toda lattice is one of the few examples of a classically integrable system with non-linear exponential coupling. My work investigated transport properties, chaos and equilibrium correlations, and hydrodynamic behaviors in Toda lattice. We use recently developed theoretical tools of integrable hydrodynamics to probe many-body equilibrium and non-equilibrium phenomena.

Anomalous Heat Transport and Fractional Diffusion
    Heat transport is phenomenologically described by the Fourier heat diffusion equation. However, this breaks down at nanoscale and there is faster than diffusion transport. My research on anomalous heat transport examines fractional diffusion equations and how they describe heat transport in low-dimensional systems.

Driven Classical systems
    A typical many-body system subject to external drive absorbs heat from the drive and heats up. In this set of work we investigate periodically and aperiodically driven many-body classical systems focusing on heating timescales and hydrodynamic description.
