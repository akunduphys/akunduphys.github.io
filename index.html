I am a Research Scientist in the Faculty of Science, Technology & Medicine at the University of Luxembourg, working at the intersection of quantum mechanics, statistical physics, and non-equilibrium systems. I currently lead a €409k FNR-CORE grant as Principal Investigator on quantum dynamics and stochastic systems.

My research investigates the dynamics of classical and quantum systems with a focus on stochastic processes. Current themes include open quantum systems, quantum measurement, and noise characterization in NISQ devices. Previously I explored integrable systems, anomalous heat transport, and fractional diffusion at SISSA, Trieste and ICTS-TIFR, Bangalore.

I hold a Ph.D. in Physics from ICTS-TIFR (2019, dissertation on analytically tractable models of one-dimensional anomalous heat transport) and an M.Sc. in Physics from IIT Kanpur (2013). I believe science is for the exploration of ideas and continuous learning, and I enjoy cross-disciplinary thinking within physics.

Stochasticity in Quantum Dynamics

Quantum dynamics subject to noise is an active field. My interest focuses on the interface of stochastic processes, quantum dynamics and quantum measurement — unravelling noisy qubit dynamics beyond the average picture. Keywords: open quantum systems, noisy quantum systems, Zeno effect, quantum trajectories.

  • Run-and-Tumble Dynamics and Zeno–Anti-Zeno Transition in Biased Quantum Trajectories. A Kundu
    We study biased quantum trajectories under continuous measurement and identify a run-and-tumble-like stochastic process governing the dynamics. A Zeno–Anti-Zeno transition is observed as a function of measurement rate, with implications for quantum control.
    arXiv:2512.20519 preprint
  • Quantum spikes in Poissonian measurement. Alan Sherry, Cedric Bernardin, Abhishek Dhar, A Kundu, Raphael Cheterite
    We investigate the statistical properties of quantum systems subject to Poissonian measurement and characterize the resulting spike-like fluctuation structures, providing a framework for understanding measurement-induced phenomena in open quantum systems.
    Phys. Rev. A 111, 042215 (2025)
  • Quantum Dynamics with Stochastic Non-Hermitian Hamiltonians. Pablo Martinez-Azcona, A Kundu, Avadh Saxena, Adolfo del Campo, Aurelia Chenu
    We study quantum systems governed by stochastic non-Hermitian Hamiltonians, developing a statistical framework for their dynamics and identifying signatures of noise that go beyond the Lindblad master equation description.
    Phys. Rev. Lett. 135, 010402 (2025)
  • Stochastic Operator Variance: an observable to diagnose noise and scrambling. P Martinez-Azcona, A Kundu, A del Campo, A Chenu
    We introduce the Stochastic Operator Variance (SOV) as a diagnostic observable that distinguishes decoherence from quantum scrambling in noisy quantum systems, providing a practical tool for quantum information experiments.
    Phys. Rev. Lett. 131, 160202 (2023)
  • Distribution of quantum discord in Heisenberg Antiferromagnets. A Kundu, V Subrahmanyam J. Phys. A: Math. Theor. 46, 435304 (2013)

Classical Integrable Systems & Hydrodynamics

The Toda lattice is one of the few classically integrable systems with non-linear exponential coupling. My work investigates transport properties, chaos, equilibrium correlations, and hydrodynamic behaviors using tools of generalised integrable hydrodynamics.

  • Integrable hydrodynamics of Toda chain: case of small systems. A Kundu Eur. Phys. J. Special Topics (2023)
  • Equilibrium dynamical correlations in the Toda chain and other integrable models. A Kundu, A Dhar
    We compute equilibrium dynamical correlations of conserved quantities in the Toda chain and compare them with predictions of generalised hydrodynamics, finding excellent agreement and establishing benchmarks for integrable transport.
    Phys. Rev. E 94, 062130 (2016)
  • Transport properties of the classical Toda chain: effect of a pinning potential. A Kundu, Abhishek Dhar, Jasen A. Scaramazza, Joel L. Lebowitz J. Stat. Phys. 176, 168 (2019)
  • Dynamical correlations of conserved quantities in the one-dimensional equal mass hard particle gas. A Kundu, Abhishek Dhar, Sanjib Sabhapandit J. Stat. Mech. (2020)
  • Revisiting the Mazur bound and the Suzuki equality. Abhishek Dhar, A Kundu, Keiji Saito Chaos, Solitons & Fractals 137 (2020)

Anomalous Heat Transport & Fractional Diffusion

At the nanoscale, Fourier's heat diffusion law breaks down and transport becomes anomalous — faster than diffusion. My work develops fractional diffusion equations as a description of heat transport in low-dimensional systems.

  • Anomalous heat transport in one-dimensional systems: A description using non-local fractional-type diffusion equation. Abhishek Dhar, Anupam Kundu, A Kundu Front. Phys. 7, 159 (2019)
  • Fractional diffusion equation description of an open anomalous heat conduction set-up. A Kundu, Cédric Bernardin, Abhishek Dhar, Anupam Kundu, Keiji Saito
    We derive and validate a fractional diffusion equation that describes anomalous heat conduction in an open one-dimensional system connected to thermal reservoirs, providing an analytical framework for non-Fourier transport phenomena.
    J. Stat. Mech. (2019)
  • Anomalous heat equation in a system connected to thermal reservoirs. Priyanka, A Kundu, A Dhar, A Kundu Phys. Rev. E 98, 042105 (2018)

Driven Classical Systems

Investigating periodically and aperiodically driven many-body classical systems, focusing on heating timescales, prethermalization, and hydrodynamic descriptions.

  • Statistical prethermalization in randomly kicked many-body classical rotor system. A Kundu, Tanay Nag, Atanu Rajak
    We study a many-body classical rotor system subject to random periodic kicks and find a regime of statistical prethermalization, where the system heats up slowly before reaching thermal equilibrium. We characterise the prethermalization timescale and its dependence on kick strength and system size, revealing universal features of heating in randomly driven classical systems.
    arXiv:2408.11109 under review — PRB
  • Dynamics of fluctuation correlation in periodically driven classical system. A Kundu, Atanu Rajak, Tanay Nag Phys. Rev. B 104, 075161 (2021)
